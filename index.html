Research Overview

From Population Genomics to Algorithmic Fairness

My research lies at the intersection of statistical genetics, bioinformatics, and computational method development. I work with large-scale genomic data — including genome-wide association studies (GWAS), bulk RNA-seq, and single-cell RNA-seq — to uncover the genetic and transcriptomic underpinnings of complex diseases such as gastric cancer, depression, nontuberculous mycobacterial pulmonary disease, and severe asthma.

A core emphasis of my work is algorithmic development: designing statistically rigorous pipelines that address the unique challenges of correlated, high-dimensional, and heterogeneous biological data. Tools I have developed include BALLI (differential expression for correlated RNA-seq) and COBRA (batch effect correction for single-cell data).

Going forward, I aim to investigate selection bias in biomedical machine learning — particularly the underrepresentation of diverse ancestries, sexes, and demographic groups in training data — and to develop debiasing algorithms that produce equitable model performance across population strata.

Research Areas

Core Research Directions

🧬
GWASGenetic Epidemiology

Population Genomics & Genetic Epidemiology

I conduct genome-wide association studies (GWAS) to identify genetic loci associated with complex diseases — including gastric cancer, depression, NTM pulmonary disease, and asthma — across large-scale population cohorts in Korea and Japan. Analyses integrate SNP data, environmental factors, and disease phenotypes to elucidate genomic risk architecture.

🔬
Bulk RNA-seqscRNA-seq

Transcriptomics & Multi-Omics

I analyze bulk RNA-seq and single-cell RNA-seq data to characterize transcriptional changes associated with disease states, drug treatments, and cell-type-specific responses. Applications include severe asthma under biologics therapy, mesenchymal stem cells, and schizophrenia models, with a focus on identifying differentially expressed genes and cell-type heterogeneity.

⚙️
AlgorithmSoftware

Statistical & Computational Method Development

I develop open-source statistical tools tailored to genomic data: BALLI (Bartlett-adjusted likelihood-based linear model for correlated RNA-seq), COBRA (cell-type-specific orthogonal batch effect removal for scRNA-seq), and extensions targeting biobank-scale correlated datasets. Emphasis is placed on statistical rigor, interpretability, and scalability.

🔭
Future Direction

Fairness & Selection Bias in Biomedical AI

My next research thrust will examine selection bias in machine learning models applied to genomic and clinical data — focusing on the underrepresentation of non-European ancestries, sex-stratified variation, and demographic minorities in training cohorts. The goal is to develop debiasing algorithms that ensure equitable model performance across diverse population strata.

I am a statistical geneticist and bioinformatician whose research spans genome-wide association studies, transcriptomics (bulk and single-cell RNA-seq), and computational method development. My work aims to develop statistically rigorous algorithms for high-dimensional genomic data and to translate findings into biological insight for complex diseases. I received my Ph.D. from Seoul National University under Prof. Sungho Won, and subsequently held research positions at the Institute of Health and Environment, Seoul National University, before joining Jeonbuk National University as Assistant Professor in September 2025.
