We are a computational genomics research group at the Department of Statistics, Jeonbuk National University.
Our lab develops statistical methods and computational pipelines for large-scale genomic data — integrating GWAS, bulk RNA-seq, and single-cell transcriptomics — to uncover the genetic and molecular basis of complex diseases.
We are committed to building methods that are statistically rigorous, biologically grounded, and equitable across diverse human populations.

Research Overview

From Population Genomics to Algorithmic Fairness

Our group develops and applies statistical and computational methods for large-scale genomic data. We work across modalities — genome-wide association studies (GWAS), bulk RNA-seq, and single-cell RNA-seq — to characterize the genetic and transcriptomic architecture of complex traits including gastric cancer, depression, nontuberculous mycobacterial pulmonary disease, and severe asthma.

A defining characteristic of our lab is a focus on algorithm development. We design statistically rigorous tools that address the unique challenges of correlated, high-dimensional, and heterogeneous biological data. Our software includes BALLI (differential expression analysis for correlated RNA-seq) and COBRA (cell-type-specific batch effect removal for single-cell data).

Going forward, we aim to investigate selection bias in biomedical machine learning — particularly the underrepresentation of diverse ancestries, sexes, and demographic groups in training data — and to develop debiasing algorithms that produce equitable model performance across population strata.

Research Areas

Core Research Directions

🧬
GWASGenetic Epidemiology

Population Genomics & Genetic Epidemiology

We conduct genome-wide association studies (GWAS) to identify genetic loci associated with complex diseases across large-scale population cohorts in Korea and Japan. Analyses integrate SNP data, environmental exposures, and disease phenotypes to elucidate genomic risk architecture for traits including gastric cancer, depression, NTM pulmonary disease, and asthma.

🔬
Bulk RNA-seqscRNA-seq

Transcriptomics & Multi-Omics

We analyze bulk and single-cell RNA-seq data to characterize transcriptional changes associated with disease states, drug treatments, and cell-type-specific responses. Applications include severe asthma under biologic therapy, mesenchymal stem cells, and neuropsychiatric disease models, with a focus on differentially expressed genes and cellular heterogeneity.

⚙️
AlgorithmSoftware

Statistical & Computational Method Development

We develop open-source statistical tools tailored to genomic data: BALLI (Bartlett-adjusted likelihood-based linear model for correlated RNA-seq), COBRA (cell-type-specific orthogonal batch effect removal for scRNA-seq), and extensions targeting biobank-scale correlated datasets. Emphasis is on statistical rigor, interpretability, and scalability.

🔭
Future Direction

Fairness & Selection Bias in Biomedical AI

Our next research thrust will examine selection bias in machine learning models applied to genomic and clinical data — focusing on underrepresentation of non-European ancestries, sex-stratified variation, and demographic minorities in training cohorts. The goal is to develop debiasing algorithms that ensure equitable model performance across diverse population strata.
