Overview

Bridging Genomics and Equitable Science

Our research sits at the intersection of statistical methods and genomics, with a commitment to equitable science.

We leverage GWAS, bulk RNA-seq, and single-cell transcriptomics to characterize the genetic and transcriptomic architecture of complex traits — and we build computational methods that are statistically rigorous, biologically grounded, and equitable by design.

Going forward, a central focus of our work will be identifying and correcting selection bias in biomedical AI — the underrepresentation of non-European ancestries and sex-stratified variation in training data leads to inequitable model performance across diverse populations. We aim to build debiasing frameworks that close this gap.

Research

Core Research Directions

⚙️
AlgorithmSoftware

Statistical Method Development

Novel statistical algorithms for high-dimensional genomic data — differential expression, batch correction, cell-type deconvolution — with emphasis on rigor and biobank-scale scalability.

🧬
GWASRNA-seq

Medical Genomics & Multi-Omics

Large-scale GWAS, bulk RNA-seq, and scRNA-seq analyses to dissect the genetic and transcriptomic underpinnings of complex traits across diverse human cohorts.

⚖️
Future Direction

Fairness & Bias Correction in Biomedical AI

Investigating selection bias in AI/ML models trained on genomic and clinical data, with focus on ancestry, sex, and demographic underrepresentation. Goal: debiasing frameworks for equitable predictions across population strata.
