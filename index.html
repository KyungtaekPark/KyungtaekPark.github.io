Overview

From Population Genomics
to Bias-Aware Machine Learning

Our research sits at the intersection of statistical genomics and algorithmic fairness.

We leverage GWAS, bulk RNA-seq, and single-cell transcriptomics to characterize the genetic and transcriptomic architecture of complex traits — and we build computational methods that are statistically rigorous, biologically grounded, and equitable by design.

🔭 Future Direction — A central focus of our upcoming work is identifying and correcting selection bias in biomedical AI: the underrepresentation of non-European ancestries and sex-stratified variation in training data leads to inequitable model performance across diverse populations. We aim to build debiasing frameworks that close this gap.

Research

Core Research Directions

🧬
GWASRNA-seq

Population Genomics & Multi-Omics

Large-scale GWAS, bulk RNA-seq, and scRNA-seq analyses to dissect the genetic and transcriptomic underpinnings of complex traits across diverse cohorts.

⚙️
AlgorithmSoftware

Statistical & Computational Methods

Novel algorithms for high-dimensional genomic data — differential expression, batch correction, cell-type deconvolution — with emphasis on statistical rigor and biobank-scale scalability.

⚖️
Future Direction

Fairness & Bias Correction in Biomedical AI

Investigating selection bias in AI/ML models trained on genomic and clinical data, with focus on ancestry, sex, and demographic underrepresentation. Goal: debiasing frameworks for equitable predictions across population strata.
